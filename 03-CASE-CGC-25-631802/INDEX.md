## Theory of the Case

This action targets CSAA Insurance Exchange's **pre-litigation false denial letter**, issued
within 21 days of the collision (before litigation was filed), which stated that CSAA had
"concluded its investigation" and determined its insured was not liable.

The word "concluded" carries a factual predicate: the investigation is finished because
the investigative steps have been performed. A reasonable recipient understands this to mean
that the basic steps of an automobile liability investigation have been performed and reviewed,
including obtaining 911 recordings, bodycam footage, and CAD records.

**CSAA had not obtained any of these materials before issuing the denial.**

Plaintiff's former counsel obtained the 911 recording through a routine public records request
within three months of the collision. It contained the defendant's own words:
*"I barely hit him with anything."*

This action is **not** a coverage or bad-faith dispute. It is a **representational-event** action:
the denial letter is treated as a fixed timestamp against which the falsity of the "concluded
investigation" representation is provable from CSAA's own claim file.

---

## The Denial Letter as Predicate Fraud

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| Date of collision | 2021 |
| Date of denial letter | 21 days after collision |
| Representation made | "Concluded our investigation"; insured not liable |
| What was done before denial | None of the foundational investigative steps |
| 911 recording | Obtained by Plaintiff's counsel within 3 months via public records request |
| CSAA's acquisition of 911 recording | Not obtained before denial |
| Body-worn camera footage | Not obtained before denial |
| CAD dispatch records | Not obtained before denial |

---

## Why This Avoids Standard Defenses

| Defense | Why It Fails Here |
|---|---|
| Litigation privilege (Civ. Code § 47(b)) | Denial letter was issued before any litigation; not a court communication |
| Anti-SLAPP (CCP § 425.16) | Gravamen is pre-litigation denial letter; post-litigation conduct pleaded only as corroboration of falsity |
| "Opinion" defense | "Concluded" is completion language, not opinion language; asserts a process was performed |
| Intent requirement | Falsity is provable from CSAA's own claim file without requiring proof of subjective intent |

## Relief Sought

1. Damages for the representational fraud in the denial letter
2. Restitution under California Unfair Competition Law (Bus. & Prof. Code § 17200 et seq.)
3. Injunctive relief requiring CSAA to conduct genuine investigations before issuing denial letters
4. Statutory and punitive damages as permitted
